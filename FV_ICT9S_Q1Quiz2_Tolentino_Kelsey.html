Loving Vincent


This links to: Photo Reference

This movie isn't your "typical" kids movie, as it tackles some topics that young children wouldn't understand. Loving Vincent introduces us a character named Joseph Roulin, a postman, who asks his son Armand to deliver Vincent Van Gogh's final letter to his brother, Theo due to Van Gogh's recent death. This movie exercises your brain, as it gets better and better, Joseph seems to be dubious and suspicious about his death. This movie also uses a different type of animation, which is called "stop-motion". In my opinion, this movie is a true gem and deserves more recognition. It teaches you a lot about life, navigating through challenges, and a lot more. Many people can interpret this movie any way they want, although most people say that Van Gogh was someone who went through difficult times in their life, thus, leading to depression.
